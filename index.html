I am a PhD Candidate in Education Policy at Harvard. My research focuses on understanding how the ways schools and classrooms are organized could enable/hinder students' economic mobility. I use descriptive, quasi-experimental and experimental methods to uncover, understand and address the sources of persistent race-, immigrant- and class-based inequality. My recent research projects have been supported by the Russell Sage Foundation, Stone Program in Wealth Distribution, Inequality, and Social Policy at Harvard and Harvard Institute for Quantitative Social Sciences.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a Research Analyst in the Education and Crime Labs at UChicago and was a middle school mathematics teacher. I hold a B.Sc. in Economics from Georgetown University and an Ed.M. in International Education Policy from Harvard.
