Welcome! I am on the job market.

My Job Market Paper

I am a PhD Candidate in Education Policy at Harvard. My research focuses on how the organization of schools and classrooms affects economic mobility. I use descriptive, quasi-experimental and experimental methods to uncover, understand and address the sources of persistent race-, immigrant- and class-based inequality. My recent research projects have been supported by the Russell Sage Foundation, Stone Program in Wealth Distribution, Inequality, and Social Policy at Harvard and Harvard Institute for Quantitative Social Sciences, and featured in the Harvard Ed. Magazine Fall 2024 issue here.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I worked as a Research Analyst in the Education and Crime Labs at the University of Chicago and was a middle school mathematics teacher. I hold a B.Sc. in Economics from Georgetown University and an Ed.M. in International Education Policy from Harvard.
